Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 19, 2026

SnapScan is a free tool that helps SNAP/EBT shoppers check whether a product is eligible under their state's 2026 restrictions. We built it to protect shoppers from embarrassment at checkout — which means we don't want your data and we've designed the product accordingly. This page explains exactly what we do and don't collect, how cookies and advertising work, and your rights.

The short version

  • We don't ask you to create an account. We don't know who you are.
  • We don't store what you scan or search for on our servers.
  • Your selected state is saved locally on your device only.
  • Location detection is optional and happens once; we don't track you over time.
  • Google AdSense may serve ads and use cookies per their own privacy policy.
  • You can deny ad personalization, delete everything, or stop using the site at any time.

1. Who we are

SnapScan is operated by Lexx Automates, a sole-proprietor business based in Coconut Creek, Florida, United States. You can reach us at lexxautomates@gmail.com for any privacy question, data access request, or deletion request. We respond within 30 days.

2. What we collect

2.1 Information you enter

When you use SnapScan, you may type brand names, UPC barcodes, or product names into the search box, and select a U.S. state. This information is processed in your browser to generate an eligibility verdict. We do not send search queries to our own servers for logging or analytics. We do send the UPC or search term to Open Food Facts so they can return product data — see Section 4 below.

3.2 Location (only if you allow it)

To auto-detect your state, SnapScan can use two optional methods:

  • IP-based approximation via a third-party service (ipapi.co). This only happens when you click "Auto-detect my state" or on first visit. Your IP address is sent to that service, which returns an approximate region. We do not receive or store your IP.
  • Browser geolocation as a fallback. This is only used if you explicitly allow the browser prompt. We compare your coordinates to state centroids locally and keep only the two-letter state code. We do not transmit your coordinates anywhere.

You can deny location access and pick your state manually at any time. We never track you over time.

3.3 Camera (only if you scan)

If you use the barcode scanner, your browser will ask permission to access the camera. The camera feed is processed entirely on your device by the ZXing open-source library. No video, image, or audio is sent to our servers or any third party. As soon as a barcode is recognized or you close the scan dialog, camera access stops.

3.4 Technical information

When you load the page, our hosting provider (Perplexity Computer, Cloudflare, or equivalent) receives standard web-server logs: IP address, browser user agent, requested URL, and timestamp. These logs are used solely for abuse prevention and operational monitoring and are typically retained for 30 days.

3.5 What we do NOT collect

  • Name, email, phone number, or address
  • SNAP/EBT account numbers or benefit balances (we never touch these — we're not connected to USDA or any state system)
  • Payment information (the consumer app is free)
  • Persistent identifiers that follow you across sites
  • Camera images or barcode scan history

3. Cookies and local storage

SnapScan itself does not set any cookies. We use your browser's localStorage to remember two small preferences:

  • snapScanState — the two-letter U.S. state code you last picked (e.g. FL)
  • snapScanLang — your language preference (en, es, or ht)

These stay on your device until you clear your browser storage. They contain no personally identifying information. If your browser blocks localStorage, SnapScan falls back to in-memory storage and you'll need to reselect your state on each visit.

Our service worker also caches the app's HTML, CSS, and JavaScript on your device so SnapScan works offline. You can clear this cache anytime from your browser settings.

Third-party cookies may be set by Google AdSense — see Section 5.

4. Third-party services

We rely on the following third-party services. Each has its own privacy policy:

  • Open Food Facts — product lookup by UPC or brand name. When you scan or search, the UPC/query is sent to their API. Open Food Facts Privacy Policy
  • ipapi.co — optional IP-based state detection. Your IP is sent to their service when you use auto-detect. ipapi.co Privacy Policy
  • Fontshare (by Indian Type Foundry) — serves the web fonts used on SnapScan. Standard web-server logs only. Fontshare Privacy Policy
  • unpkg (Cloudflare) — CDN delivery of the ZXing barcode library. Standard web-server logs only. Cloudflare Privacy Policy
  • Google AdSense — serves advertising (see Section 5).

5. Advertising (Google AdSense)

SnapScan is free to use. To cover hosting and development costs, we display ads served by Google AdSense. Here's how that works and what it means for you:

  • Google and its advertising partners use cookies, device identifiers, and similar technologies to serve ads based on your prior visits to this and other websites. This is called "personalized advertising."
  • Google uses the DoubleClick DART cookie and similar technologies to do this.
  • You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.
  • You can opt out of third-party vendor use of cookies for personalized ads by visiting www.aboutads.info/choices or, if in the EU, www.youronlinechoices.eu.
  • For more information about how Google uses data from partner sites and apps, see policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.
  • Google's advertising cookies and policies are governed by the Google Privacy Policy.

If you're in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or Switzerland, SnapScan shows you a consent banner (powered by Google's EU User Consent framework) before loading personalized ads. If you decline, you'll see only non-personalized, context-based ads.

We do not share your search queries, scanned UPCs, selected state, or any SnapScan usage data with Google or any advertiser.

6. Children's privacy

SnapScan is a general-audience tool and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided information, contact us and we'll delete it.

7. Your rights

7.1 Everyone

You can at any time:

  • Clear SnapScan's locally stored data by clearing your browser's site data for our domain
  • Revoke camera or location permissions from your browser settings
  • Stop using the site

7.2 EU / UK / EEA (GDPR)

If you are in the EU, UK, or EEA, you have the right to:

  • Access, correct, or delete the personal data we process about you
  • Object to processing, including for direct marketing
  • Request restriction of processing
  • Data portability (a copy of your data in a machine-readable format)
  • Withdraw consent at any time (where consent is the legal basis)
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority

Because we don't collect identifying information, we usually have nothing to access or delete. The main legal basis for our limited processing is our legitimate interest in providing the service and, where applicable, your consent (e.g., for location access or personalized advertising).

7.3 California (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect (this policy)
  • Request deletion of personal information
  • Opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising
  • Not be discriminated against for exercising these rights

SnapScan itself does not sell personal information. Google AdSense's serving of personalized ads may qualify as "sharing" under CPRA; you can opt out via the banner or by enabling Global Privacy Control (GPC) in your browser — we honor GPC signals.

To exercise any right, email lexxautomates@gmail.com.

8. Data security

SnapScan is served over HTTPS. Because we don't store user data on our servers, there's no user database to breach. The retailer-facing API (a separate product for grocers and POS vendors) does store hashed API keys and usage counts in Supabase; that side has its own privacy considerations and is not used by consumers.

9. International transfers

Our services and the third parties we use (Google, Cloudflare, etc.) operate globally. If you access SnapScan from outside the United States, your interaction with those services may involve transferring data to the U.S. or other countries. The third parties listed above maintain safeguards (SCCs, UK IDTA, etc.) where required.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy to reflect changes in our practices or in applicable law. We'll update the "Last updated" date above and, for material changes, add a notice on the homepage for at least 30 days.

11. Contact

Questions or requests? Email lexxautomates@gmail.com.

SnapScan es operado por Lexx Automates, un negocio unipersonal con sede en Coconut Creek, Florida, EE.UU. Puedes contactarnos en lexxautomates@gmail.com para cualquier pregunta de privacidad, solicitud de acceso a datos o solicitud de eliminación. Respondemos en un plazo de 30 días.

2. Qué recopilamos

2.1 Información que ingresas

Al usar SnapScan, puedes escribir marcas, códigos UPC o nombres de productos en el buscador, y elegir un estado de EE.UU. Esta información se procesa en tu navegador para generar un veredicto. No enviamos tus búsquedas a nuestros servidores para registro o análisis. Sí enviamos el UPC o término de búsqueda a Open Food Facts para que devuelvan los datos del producto — ver Sección 4.

2.2 Ubicación (solo si lo permites)

Para detectar tu estado automáticamente, SnapScan puede usar dos métodos opcionales:

  • Aproximación por IP mediante un servicio externo (ipapi.co). Solo ocurre cuando haces clic en "Detectar mi estado" o en tu primera visita. Tu dirección IP se envía a ese servicio, que devuelve una región aproximada. Nosotros no recibimos ni almacenamos tu IP.
  • Geolocalización del navegador como respaldo. Solo se usa si aceptas el aviso del navegador. Comparamos tus coordenadas con centros de estados localmente y solo conservamos el código de dos letras. No transmitimos tus coordenadas a ninguna parte.

Puedes rechazar la ubicación y elegir tu estado manualmente en cualquier momento. Nunca te rastreamos con el tiempo.

2.3 Cámara (solo si escaneas)

Si usas el escáner, tu navegador pedirá permiso para acceder a la cámara. La imagen se procesa totalmente en tu dispositivo con la librería de código abierto ZXing. No se envía video, imagen ni audio a nuestros servidores ni a terceros. Al reconocer un código o cerrar el escáner, el acceso a la cámara se detiene.

2.4 Información técnica

Al cargar la página, nuestro proveedor de hosting recibe registros estándar: IP, navegador, URL solicitada y marca de tiempo. Se usan solo para prevenir abuso y monitorear, y se retienen típicamente 30 días.

2.5 Lo que NO recopilamos

  • Nombre, email, teléfono o dirección
  • Números de cuenta SNAP/EBT ni saldos (nunca los tocamos — no estamos conectados con USDA ni ningún sistema estatal)
  • Información de pago (la app para consumidores es gratis)
  • Identificadores persistentes que te siguen entre sitios
  • Imágenes de cámara ni historial de escaneos

3. Cookies y almacenamiento local

SnapScan no coloca cookies propias. Usamos el localStorage del navegador para recordar dos preferencias pequeñas:

  • snapScanState — el código de estado que elegiste (ej. FL)

Permanecen en tu dispositivo hasta que borres los datos del sitio. No contienen información identificable. Si tu navegador bloquea localStorage, SnapScan usa memoria temporal y tendrás que reseleccionar el estado cada visita.
